Midnight Harbor

An original short English poem — static single-file HTML

The quay remembers footsteps like small ships— soft arrivals that wake the salt and gutters. A lantern rocks the hush; a gull folds its wing and the harbor keeps the quiet it promised. You place your shadow on the rail: a pause, then breathe the map of sea and stitched lights. A bell far off counts one less than the hour, and the night holds this small, honest forgetting.
